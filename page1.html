Part1: Planning

by Sirtaj Singh

A magic mirror uses a Raspberry pi as the brains, a two-way mirror and monitor. Together, it can act as a mirror and a grear source of information. This is what my end goal is like. Source.

When I got a Raspberry pi 3 I didn’t exactly know what I wanted to do with it. The options for what I could do where endless; a streaming device, an emulator, an ad-blocker and much more.

I didn’t actually do much with it for a while other than turn it on and play around with it. Then I saw a blog post about a Magic Mirror powered with a Raspberry pi. It was amazing. So that’s the direction I decided to go with.

In order to build my mirror, I needed to plan and get a few things:

  • Raspberry pi 3 to upload the software
  • A monitor, preferably >24′, to display the info
  • A two-way mirror, the real magic
  • Wood to make the frame
  • Screws, cables etc.

My goal is to finish the programming in one week, and finish all the hardware in the next week. Assembly should only take a few days. The hardest part I think will be finding a two-way mirror in my area and managing the wiring. Click on Project Menu to navigate through my project.
